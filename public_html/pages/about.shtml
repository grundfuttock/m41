Why

I have over forty years experience in the IT industry, from system level device-drivers to enterprise sales through consultancy, project and line management

Background

Following my degree in Computation at Manchester university, I have been working in IT since 1976. From writing industrial control systems in assembler through project management to sales and team management. For more details, a copy of of my CV is available to view or download.

In 2005 I left IT and ran my own (non-IT related) business.

More recently I have rediscovered the joy in developing solutions that are elegant, simple and effective.

As an aside, a comparison of programming in the 1970s to the current day shows that, while complexity has increased significantly, the toolset available has improved immeasurably.

Another significant change is in the number of people employed in development and a new culture of openness. Although this has changed the pioneering nature of programming to one of building from existing components, the underlying joy of blending order with creativity remains.

Since 2013 I have been complementing my IT skills with volunteer work for Trafford council, providing advice on Digital Inclusion and delivering introductory IT sessions to Trafford residents.
